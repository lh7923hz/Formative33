WINONA STATE UNIVERSITY 


Overview
Winona State University is a public institution that was founded in 1858. 
It has a total undergraduate enrollment of 5,763 (fall 2021), its setting is city, and the campus size is 125 acres. 
It utilizes a semester-based academic calendar.

Winona State University admissions is selective with an acceptance rate of 77%. 
Half the applicants admitted to Winona State have an SAT score between 970 and 1280 or an ACT score of 19 and 25. 
However, one quarter of admitted applicants achieved scores above these ranges and one quarter scored below these ranges. 
The application deadline is July 16 and the application fee at Winona State University is $20.

Admissions officials at Winona State University consider a student's GPA a very important academic factor.
An applicant's high school class rank, when available, is considered important but letters of recommendation are not considered by admissions officials at Winona State University
